Scenario 1

You're frustrated that an employee's work that is consistently not meeting the required standard. You decide to assign them only tedious, low-impact tasks and stop inviting them to key team meetings, hoping they'll "get the message." The employee resigns a week later.

Risk: Constructive Dismissal

What it is: When an employerâ€™s actions or conduct force an employee to resign, such as creating an intolerable environment (e.g., unfair reviews, unreasonable demands, or a hostile workplace).

Your mitigation checklist:

  • Am I genuinely addressing this employee's feedback and concerns?
  • Am I maintaining open, professional, and respectful communication?
  • Am I actively excluding this team member from key activities (meetings, projects) in a way that could be seen as isolating them?
  • Am I following guidance on managing underperformance, as set out in Dealing with Underperformance - People Leader Guide?

Scenario 2

During a performance review, you mention to an older employee that they "seem less energetic" than the younger team members. Their performance rating is lower than their younger peers, despite having similar output.

Risk: Discrimination

What it is: Making performance-related decisions based on protected characteristics (e.g., age, gender, race, disability). This can include biased feedback, unequal standards, or discriminatory outcomes.

Your mitigation checklist:

  • Is my feedback 100% objective and based on behaviour and output, not personality or subjective traits?
  • Am I applying the exact same standards and role-specific performance criteria to all team members equally, when they are in the same type of role?
  • Am I maintaining open communication and addressing concerns promptly and fairly if raised?

Scenario 3

An employee, who is usually a high performer, suddenly starts missing deadlines and seems disengaged, but has also shared with you that they are experiencing wellbeing challenges. You immediately schedule a performance discussion and plan to focus solely on their drop in output.

Risk: Wellbeing Concerns

What it is: Risks to employee wellbeing caused or worsened by performance management, such as stress or burnout. Sometimes, wellbeing issues may make it temporarily unfair to expect good performance.

Your mitigation checklist:

  • Am I leading the discussion with empathy first? (e.g., "I've noticed... is everything okay?")
  • Am I ready to proactively offer support, like the EAP or a temporary workload adjustment, and ensure I share this in writing?
  • Should I consult with PX for guidance before and performance conversations, given the sudden change?
