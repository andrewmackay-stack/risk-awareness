Scenario 1

You're frustrated with an employee's consistently poor work. You decide to assign them only tedious, low-impact tasks and stop inviting them to key team meetings, hoping they'll "get the message." The employee resigns a week later.

Risk: Constructive Dismissal

What it is: When an employer’s actions or conduct force an employee to resign, such as creating an intolerable environment (e.g., unfair reviews, unreasonable demands, or a hostile workplace).

Your Mitigation Checklist

  • Am I genuinely addressing this employee's feedback and concerns, rather than trying to "manage them out"?
  • Am I maintaining open, professional, and respectful communication?
  • Am I fostering a positive and inclusive environment for all team members?
  • Am I following fair procedures and all company policies for performance management?

Scenario 2

During a performance review, you mention to an older employee that they "seem less energetic" than the younger team members. Their performance rating is lower than a junior colleague's, despite having similar output.

Risk: Discrimination

What it is: Making performance-related decisions based on protected characteristics (e.g., age, gender, race, disability). This can include biased feedback, unequal standards, or discriminatory outcomes.

Your Mitigation Checklist

  • Is my feedback 100% objective and based *only* on clear, measurable criteria and defined job responsibilities?
  • Am I applying the exact same standards and criteria to all team members equally?
  • Is my documentation clear, factual, and completely free of biased language, assumptions, or personal opinions?
  • Am I addressing concerns promptly and fairly if an employee raises them?

Scenario 3

An employee, who is usually a high performer, suddenly starts missing deadlines and seems disengaged. You immediately schedule a "Formal Performance Discussion" and plan to focus solely on their drop in output.

Risk: Wellbeing Concerns

What it is: Risks to employee wellbeing caused or worsened by performance management, such as stress or burnout. Sometimes, wellbeing issues may make it temporarily unfair to expect good performance.

Your Mitigation Checklist

  • Am I leading the discussion with empathy first, before jumping to performance metrics? (e.g., "I've noticed... is everything okay?")
  • Am I ready to proactively offer support, like the EAP, and remind them of available resources?
  • Have I considered whether a temporary workload adjustment or other support is needed?
  • Will I clearly offer this support in writing (e.g., in a follow-up email) to document it?
  • Should I consult with PX for guidance *before* this meeting, given the sudden change?
